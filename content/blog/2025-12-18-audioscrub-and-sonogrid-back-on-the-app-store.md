My iOS apps are [on the App Store](https://apps.apple.com/developer/rcreativ/id356609408) again; if you ever wanted to have sonogrid or the others, now might be a good time to get a copy.

## [AudioScrub](https://apps.apple.com/app/audioscrub-remix-edition/id903693369)

slow down music, change keys, loop.

essential tool for transcribers, improvisers, music students.

also available with a subset of those features as a [PLAY Edition](https://apps.apple.com/app/audioscrub-play-edition/id524438615) that remote controls Apple's Music app.

I wrote in [going fully web](https://rosano.ca/blog/going-fully-web/) about my reasons to take them down and, although I continue to feel similarly, was recently convinced by [Heddi](https://ried.studio) to consider restoring them there.

Here's what motivated me:

1. People who previously paid for them could not get a copy for new or other devices. I thought this would be possible even when not officially on the store, but it turns out to be more complicated than that. After contacting those who had issues about this years ago, many quickly replied back with very visible gratitude to have access to AudioScrub again; seems it still fits their needs better than whatever else is available.

2. I worked hard on these and remain quite proud of them; not sure if it accomplishes much to nuke my history in the way that I did. Even though they were built for another era, everything still works great and I've never stopped using them.

3. It's been quite a puzzle to create income from pure web apps with my ideal structure of [no personal data stored or collected](https://0data.app), [no login or signup to start](https://rosano.ca/blog/going-doorless/), ideally [open-source](https://github.com/rosano/rosano/blob/master/README.md). While I figure out how to make that sustainable, it's helpful to have some other income streams.

No plans to update these for the moment, but I think it's a good step to keep it available.
