I've been interested to understand my family history for some years now. I already put some effort into talking with relatives, taking notes from our conversations, finding old documents, and consolidating information from different places.

There are many platforms or apps that can visually represent your tree. Using my family as an example (with anonymized names), below are some visualizations in the form of:

All of these look nice and are fun to explore but become harder to read as data accumulates. None of them help me orient to where people fit in the larger picture. So I made my own with the following characteristics:

1. radial layout to maximize space and group by generation;

<figure>

![](cosmos-1.jpg)

</figure>

2. lines to connect siblings, spouses, and children;

<figure>

![](cosmos-2.jpg)

</figure>

3. colour to roughly track lineage based on last names;

<figure>

![](cosmos-3.jpg)

</figure>

This finally gives me a sense of who my second and third cousins are, which I wasn't able to grasp before.

I also learned that surnames aren't the ultimate indicator of 'where one comes from'. Even though I commonly think of my last name as representing family origins, it really just emphasizes those who passed on *their* name; feels cool to realize that as one person I actually come from people with thirteen unique last names!

Another interesting set of trivia has to do with the Mormon Church. I never paid much attention to them, but through this work I've learned that they contribute heavily to family tree technologies and to ordinary people learning about their own family histories:
- they invented the [GEDCOM file format](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GEDCOM) in 1984, which all these apps use to import and export genealogical data
- they probably run a '[FamilySearch Center](https://www.familysearch.org/en/locations/centers/about)' near you to provide research help and free access to paid platforms
- their [FamilySearch wiki](https://www.familysearch.org/en/wiki/Main_Page) has about 150,000 articles to help you find vital records for family research in most countries
- they've helped archive and digitize birth, death, and other records from all over the world (accessible through their [FamilySearch](www.familysearch.org) platform or local centers)

There's more I'd like to do with this, but for now I'm happy just to have my own tree and share it with family.

## resources

If you're interested to explore deeper, I've compiled links from my research to save you some searching.

### build family trees
- [Genea](https://www.genea.app) ([open-source](https://github.com/genea-app/genea-app), privacy-oriented)
- [Family Chart](https://donatso.github.io/family-chart-doc/) ([open-source](https://github.com/donatso/family-chart), privacy-oriented)
- [Visual Family Tree](https://jisco.me/visual-family-tree/): Windows, macOS, Linux ([open-source](https://github.com/Jisco/VisualFamilyTree), privacy-oriented)

### visualize family trees
- [Topola Genealogy Viewer](https://pewu.github.io/topola-viewer/) ([open-source](https://github.com/PeWu/topola-viewer), privacy-oriented)
- [Family Tree Map](https://www.alexandraulsh.com/family-tree-map/): "Map the birthplaces of people in your family tree." ([open-source](https://github.com/alulsh/family-tree-map), privacy-oriented)
- [blood lines](https://blood-lines.ohkayblanket.com): 3D force-directed graph ([open-source](https://github.com/oh-kay-blanket/blood-lines), privacy-oriented)
- [tmcw/gedcom/live](https://tmcw.github.io/gedcom/live/): 2D force-directed graph ([open-source](https://github.com/tmcw/gedcom), privacy-oriented)
- [Exploring Family Trees](https://learnforeverlearn.com/ancestors/): tree layout on a timeline
- [arbre](https://mon.arbre.app): visualize GEDCOM data

### self-hosted genealogy tools
- [Gramps Web](https://www.grampsweb.org): collaborate with your family in a private space
- [Betty](https://bartfeenstra.github.io/betty-demo): generate an encyclopedia-like website from Gramps or GEDCOM data
- [Open Pedigree](https://github.com/phenotips/open-pedigree): draw genomic pedigrees
- [Genealogy](https://github.com/MGeurts/genealogy)
- [terrywbrady/TNG_Docker](https://github.com/terrywbrady/TNG_Docker)

### hosted genealogy tools
- build family trees: [Family Echo](https://www.familyecho.com), [Kintree](https://kintree.app), [FamilyBushes](https://familybushes.com)
- make genograms: [Genogram Creator](https://qwoach.com/tools/genogram), [GenoFlow](https://genogramm-vite.vercel.app)
- make pedigree charts: [pedigreejs](https://ccge-boadicea.github.io/pedigreejs/) ([open-source](https://github.com/CCGE-BOADICEA/pedigreejs), privacy-oriented)
